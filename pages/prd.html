On the Pseudo-Random Distribution

The pseudo-random distribution (PRD) is a probabilistic system ported to Dota 2 from the Warcraft 3 engine (ca. 2012). It represents an improvement over traditional forms of randomness in digital games.

When we think of randomness in games, we typically think of independent die rolls determining the outcomes of events. Independent rolls possess some useful properties for game development:

  • Easy to balance
  • Easy to reason about
  • Simple to implement in both analog and digital titles

They also possess a particularly annoying property, in that there's no guarantee of success (or failure) within any number of trials. Hot and cold streaks are an intrinsic part of the system.

The PRD addresses this by

The probability for a given trial is calculated as:

P(N) = C * N
  • C: pre-calculated float value
  • N: integer representing the nth trial
